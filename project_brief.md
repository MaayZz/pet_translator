# Project Brief: Pet Translation Device 🐾

## 1. Context and Main Objective
This project is part of the Machine Learning (ML01) course, Step 2. It corresponds to **Topic 4: Fun & Experimental**.
The goal is to design a (virtual/conceptual) wearable device to attach to a pet's collar (cat or dog). This device must be capable of capturing the sounds emitted by the animal, analyzing them, and "translating" them into text or voice messages sent to the owner's smartphone.

## 2. Project Expectations
The professor is looking for creativity, advanced experimentation, and deep engineering thought, going far beyond simply using existing APIs. The project must merge several domains:
- **Audio Analysis:** Capture and properly isolate the pet's voice in potentially noisy environments.
- **Classification Models:** Categorize the sound (hunger, anger, seeking attention, fear, etc.).
- **Behavioral Interpretation:** Match the sound with the supposed body language.
- **LLM System (Large Language Model):** Take the raw intent of the animal and generate a natural, funny, or sarcastic human sentence to simulate a real conversation.
- **Edge AI & Mobile:** Run some or all of the model locally for low latency, featuring a "chat" interface on a smartphone.

## 3. Implementation Guidelines
1. **Intensive AI Usage:** AI agents must be at the core of the development process (architecture assistance, code generation, debugging).
2. **Scientific and Engineering Approach:** Explore the current limitations of models, study the trade-offs between a lightweight phone model (Edge AI) and a heavy remote server model, and analyze unexpected behaviors.
3. **Required Quality:** This project must demonstrate a high standard of quality, both in terms of research and software engineering.
4. **Final Deliverables to Keep in Mind:**
   - A fun and technical video of at least 10 minutes (in English).
   - Clean, well-commented source code.
   - Accurate tracking of contributions on GitHub.
